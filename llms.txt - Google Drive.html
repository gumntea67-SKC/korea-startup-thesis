      # Korea Startup Thesis

> Weekly deep dives into Korean startups for global VC and angel investors.
> Data-driven analysis, founder stories, competitive positioning, and deal flow intelligence.

## About

Korea Startup Thesis is an English-language investment newsletter covering the Korean startup ecosystem from a global investor's perspective. Each issue features one Company Deep Dive — a rigorous, data-backed analysis of a high-potential Korean startup — plus a "Ones to Watch" radar and a Deal Tracker of recent funding rounds.

The target reader is a global VC partner, angel investor, or LP who wants signal, not noise, on Korea's most compelling companies.

## Issue Archive

- [Issue #001 — Rebellions: The Ex-SpaceX Engineer Who Came Back to Korea to Challenge Nvidia](https://koreastartupthesis.com/rebellions-issue-001)
  Published: 2026-06-14
  Tags: AI Semiconductor, ASIC, Inference, Pre-IPO, Founded 2020
  Summary: Rebellions is a Korean fabless AI chip startup that has built an inference-only ASIC (REBEL-Quad) that outperforms Nvidia's H200 on power efficiency by 20.7%. The company has raised $850M in total with a $2.34B pre-IPO valuation. ARM made Rebellions its first startup investment in APAC. Founder Park Sung-hyun's background spans KAIST → MIT PhD → Intel Labs → Samsung → SpaceX → Morgan Stanley VP before returning to Korea in 2020.

## Newsletter Structure

Each weekly issue contains:
1. **Company Deep Dive** — In-depth analysis of one Korean startup (founder story, technology analysis, competitive moat, global demand thesis, risks, key metrics to watch)
2. **Ones to Watch** — 2–3 early-stage Korean startups worth monitoring
3. **Deal Tracker** — Recent Korean startup funding rounds with context
